How to Get There

Implementation timeline

A phased approach to restructuring — no rush, no panic, tax-efficient.

Month 1 — Immediate
Stop & Start SIPs
Stop SIPs in all 6 exit funds. Start new SIPs in S&P 500 Index, Balanced Advantage, Short Term Debt, and Gold funds. Adjust existing SIP amounts for HDFC Flexi, Invesco, Bandhan.
Months 2-6 — Gradual
Redeem & Redeploy
Stagger redemptions from exit funds over 3-6 months. Use STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) to move redeemed amounts into debt and gold. Stay within ₹1.25L LTCG exemption per financial year where possible.
Ongoing — Every Year
Review & Rebalance
Annual review meeting. If any asset class drifts more than 5% from target, rebalance. Every 2 years, shift 5% from equity to debt as part of the glide path.

Key Transition Rules

Don't redeem all at once
Stagger to manage tax and timing risk

New money should go to new allocation from day 1

For the Meeting

Discussion points for Mr. Sajjan

Key questions to address in the portfolio review meeting.

01

Why 9 equity-heavy funds for a 50+ moderate-risk investor?

The current allocation (92% equity, all highly correlated) is suitable for an aggressive 30-year-old — not this client profile. What was the rationale?

02

Why was DSP Banking added when financials were already at 28%?

Adding a dedicated banking fund on top of existing financial services exposure created dangerous sector concentration. Was this a conscious overweight bet?

03

Why no debt, gold, or international allocation?

These are standard components in any balanced portfolio for someone approaching retirement. What's the plan for downside protection?

04

What rebalancing schedule is being followed?

The hypothetical report shows no rebalancing was applied. Without annual rebalancing, the portfolio drifts further from any target allocation over time.

05

How will the transition be managed tax-efficiently?

We need a clear plan for staggered redemptions, STP usage, and staying within LTCG exemption limits across financial years.
