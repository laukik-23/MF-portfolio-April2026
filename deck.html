What To Do

6 changes to build a stronger portfolio

01

Cut from 9 funds to 5-6

Keep HDFC Flexi Cap, Invesco Mid Cap, Bandhan Small Cap as core. Remove highly correlated duplicates like SBI Contra (0.95 correlation with HDFC Flexi).

02

Add bonds / fixed income (15-20%)

A short-duration or dynamic bond fund acts as a shock absorber during equity crashes, reducing overall portfolio volatility.

03

Add gold (5-10%)

Gold typically rises when stocks fall. A gold ETF or fund adds a layer of protection that this portfolio completely lacks.

04

Remove DSP Banking & Financial

The portfolio already has 28% in financials from other funds. A dedicated banking fund creates dangerous sector concentration.

05

Add international exposure (10-15%)

A US or global equity fund reduces single-country risk. When India underperforms, other markets can compensate.

06

Rebalance annually

Set a schedule to review and rebalance the portfolio yearly. This locks in gains and maintains your target allocation.

Recommendations based on: CFA Institute diversification principles · Morningstar portfolio diversification · Morgan Stanley asset allocation

  • Conservative or moderate risk appetite

  • Uncomfortable with concentrated equity exposure
  • Approaching retirement or needs stable income
Bottom line: The portfolio has delivered great returns, but it's running on a single engine with no backup. The recommendations above don't mean selling everything — they mean adding safety systems so the strong returns can continue with less risk of a painful crash.
