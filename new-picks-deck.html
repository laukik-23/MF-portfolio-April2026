The risk with sector funds

  • If a sector underperforms for years, the fund has nowhere to hide
  • Diversified funds can rotate out of weak sectors; sector funds can't
  • 3 of 4 are cyclical sectors — they amplify economic downturns
  • For a moderate-risk investor: max 1 sector fund is typical

Our recommendation

  • Remove DSP Banking — financials are already over-represented
  • Remove HDFC Transport — unnecessary cyclical bet
  • Remove Bank of India Mfg — correlated and cyclical
  • Whiteoak Pharma can stay — healthcare is defensive

Why EM doesn't diversify India well

  • Emerging markets are correlated during crises. When global investors pull out of EM, they pull out of ALL EM — India, China, Brazil all fall together.
  • No currency hedge. EM currencies weaken together against the dollar during stress.
  • We recommended S&P 500 / US equities — developed markets have genuinely different economic cycles from India.
  • S&P 500 has delivered 12-14% annualized in INR terms over 20 years — vs 4.8% from this EM fund over 5 years.
Diversifying from India into other emerging markets is like diversifying from one monsoon-dependent farm by buying another monsoon-dependent farm in the next village. When the rain fails, both fail. You need a farm in a different climate zone.

What Should Change

6 fixes to make these picks work

01

Bring back HDFC Flexi Cap as core

Every portfolio needs an anchor. HDFC Flexi Cap's diversified, low-volatility profile makes it the obvious choice. Without it, the portfolio is all satellite bets.

02

Add a dedicated debt fund (20%+)

A short-duration debt fund (e.g., HDFC Short Term Debt) is non-negotiable for a 50+ investor. This is the biggest missing piece — still.

03

Replace Edelweiss EM with S&P 500 index

Motilal Oswal S&P 500 Index Fund gives true diversification — developed market, USD exposure, different economic cycle. The EM fund underperforms and doesn't diversify India risk.

04

Remove 3 sector funds (keep Pharma)

Drop DSP Banking, HDFC Transport, Bank of India Mfg. These are correlated cyclical bets. Whiteoak Pharma can stay as the one defensive sector allocation.

05

Drop silver, keep gold

Gold is a proven safe haven. Silver is volatile, partly industrial, and doesn't protect in a crisis. One commodity allocation (gold) is sufficient.

06

Remove SBI Contra

Still highly correlated with everything in the portfolio (0.85-0.92 with all other equity funds). Adds duplication without diversification.

Bottom Line

The verdict: a lateral move

The advisor acknowledged the gaps but didn't fix the fundamentals. Our original recommendation still stands.

What the advisor got right

  • Added gold exposure (DSP Gold ETF)
  • Added international exposure (concept, not execution)
  • Added multi-asset fund for built-in diversification
  • Added defensive sector exposure (pharma)

What still needs fixing

  • Dropped the best core fund (HDFC Flexi Cap)
  • Kept all highly correlated problem funds
  • Went from 9 to 12 funds (we said 5-6)
  • Still essentially zero debt allocation
  • EM international doesn't diversify India risk
  • Silver adds volatility, not stability
  • 4 thematic sector funds is excessive
The core message hasn't changed: This client is a 50-55 year old moderate-risk investor with dependents and a cyclical income. He needs a portfolio that protects him during downturns, not one that amplifies them. The additions are cosmetic improvements on a structure that still doesn't match his profile.
